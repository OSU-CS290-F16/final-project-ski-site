Mount Hood

Mount Hood is one of the largest Mountains in the Cascade Range standing at a whopping 11,250 feet it is both taller than Mount Bachelor and Mount Ashland. It is in fact the tallest Mountain in Oregon! This Mountain has an average snowfall of over 400 inches! Wow thats a lot of snow! In addition, it holds 4 notable ski Resorts! More than Bachelor and Ashland Combined! This Mountain is the most popular skiing sanctuary in Oregon! Furthermore, it is also known to be a dormant volcano, however, it may still blow in the future so if you decide to go skiing on this mountain Watch Out!

This is a trail map of Mount Hood at Meadows.

Mount Hood is also Home to many ski resorts:

  • Mount Hood Meadows
  • Ski Bowl
  • Timberline Lodge
  • Cooper Spur

Both Meadows and Ski Bowl hold beginner, intermediate and expert level runs. So no matter what level of skiing you may be at they will have a run for you! Timberline on the other hand offers beginner and intermediate level runs but not expert while Cooper Spur offers lower level intermediate and beginner runs. The base levels vary from site to site with Cooper Spur and Ski Bowl starting at around 3500 ft while Meadows base is 4523ft and Timberline's is 6000 ft. Besides the nuanced differences between the resorts, they all house very decorative and extravagant lodging coupled with excellent skiing and views.
